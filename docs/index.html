Ctrl + L from anywhere

Everything it does, and nothing it doesn't

Lingo is one popup and a shortcut. No dashboard, no history to manage, no window to keep track of.

One shortcut, anywhere

Ctrl + L opens the popup over any app - browser, editor, chat, PDF.

Grabs your selection

Highlight text first and it's pre-filled - translation starts the instant the popup appears.

Live as you type

No selection? Just type. Translation updates line by line while you write.

A dictionary for words

Single words get senses grouped by part of speech, with alternatives and definitions.

Detects & flips scripts

Auto-detects the source language and lays out right-to-left scripts like Hebrew and Arabic correctly.

Google in, DeepL optional

Google works out of the box, key-free. Prefer DeepL? Drop in your own key.

Pin when you need it

Normally it closes the moment you click away. Pin it to keep it open while you work.

Light, dark & accents

Follows your Windows theme, and you pick the accent color that fits your setup.

A note on installing

The installer isn't code-signed yet - signing certificates are expensive for a free project. Windows SmartScreen may show a blue warning the first time you run it. Click More info, then Run anyway. Everything is open source, so you can read exactly what it does on GitHub before you trust it.
